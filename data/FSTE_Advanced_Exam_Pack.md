## 🔴 BLOCK 1: Systems Thinking & Iceberg Model (Tricky)

---

**Q1. A city's traffic management team observes "increased congestion on MG Road every Monday morning." In Iceberg Model terminology, this observation is classified as:**

A. A Pattern + Trend
B. An Underlying Structure
C. An Event
D. A Mental Model

✅ **Correct Answer: C**
**Trap Explanation:** Students confuse "recurring Monday congestion" with a Pattern. But a Pattern requires behaviour *over time* (consistent or prolonged tendency). A single observed occurrence at a specific moment = Event. If you say "congestion happens every Monday for the past 3 years," THEN it becomes a Pattern. Here the team merely "observes" it — that's an Event. The word "observes" is the trigger.

---

**Q2. "Traffic is heavy on MG Road every Monday for the last 2 years, and it seems to be worsening." This statement corresponds to which Iceberg level?**

A. Event
B. Mental Model
C. Underlying Structure
D. Pattern + Trend

✅ **Correct Answer: D**
**Trap Explanation:** Now it's recurring (2 years), consistent, prolonged → Pattern + Trend. This is NOT an event (single occurrence). The words "every Monday" + "for 2 years" + "worsening" = classic Pattern definition from notes: "consistent or prolonged tendency or behaviour."

---

**Q3. The notes say "priority increases by level" in the Iceberg Model. This means:**

A. Events are the most important level to address because they are visible
B. Mental Models are the least important because they are hidden
C. The deeper the level, the higher the priority for systems intervention — intervening at the structure/mental model level creates more lasting change than reacting to events
D. Priority increases means Events should be addressed before Patterns

✅ **Correct Answer: C**
**Trap Explanation:** Counterintuitive! "Priority increases by level" means deeper = more important for INTERVENTION, not more visible. Most people react to Events (fire-fighting). Systems thinkers intervene at Patterns, Structures, and Mental Models — creating lasting change. Events are LEAST priority for systemic change despite being most visible.

**Q13. In the Global Warming positive feedback loop described in the notes, the sequence is:**

A. Temp↑ → Ice melts → CO₂↑ → Albedo↑ → More Ice↑
B. Temp↑ → Ice melts → Albedo↓ → Heat Absorption↑ → Temp↑ (reinforcing loop)
C. CO₂↑ → Photosynthesis↑ → Biomass↑ → CO₂↓ (balancing loop)
D. Temp↑ → Rain↑ → River levels↑ → Flooding (linear chain)

✅ **Correct Answer: B**
**Trap Explanation:** Notes draw this exact loop: "+ve feedback loop → Temp↑ → Albedo↓ → Ice Melting↑ → Heat Absorption↑ → Temp↑." Albedo = reflectivity. Less ice = less reflection = more heat absorbed = temperature rises further. Option A has Albedo↑ which is wrong (less ice = less reflectivity = Albedo DECREASES).

**Q14. The negative feedback loop for CO₂ and photosynthesis from the notes shows:**

A. CO₂↑ → Plants die → Less CO₂ absorbed → CO₂↑ further (reinforcing)
B. CO₂↑ → Photosynthesis↑ → Biomass↑ → CO₂ absorbed↑ → CO₂↓ (balancing loop growing toward stability)
C. CO₂↑ → Temperature↑ → Ice melts → CO₂ absorbed (linear)
D. CO₂↑ → Photosynthesis↓ → CO₂ absorbed↓ → CO₂↑ (reinforcing)

✅ **Correct Answer: B**
**Trap Explanation:** Notes show: "CO₂↑ → Photosynthesis↑ → Biomass↑ → CO₂ absorbed↑ → CO₂↓ — grows towards stability (-ve feedback loop, balance, good)." This is a classic balancing loop — CO₂ increase triggers more plant growth which absorbs CO₂, pulling it back down.

---

**Q15. A manager gives Ken more support → Ken's performance improves → manager gives even more support → performance improves further. This describes:**

A. A Balancing loop (B) — "The Great Stabilizer"
B. A Reinforcing loop (R) — "Virtuous/Vicious Growth Cycle"
C. A Delay in feedback
D. A system in Dynamic Equilibrium

✅ **Correct Answer: B**
**Trap Explanation:** Notes: "Virtuous (Growth) cycle: supervisor supports Ken → Ken's performance → even more support." This is a Reinforcing loop — each cycle amplifies the last. Now flip it: less support → performance↓ → support drops further → "Vicious (Collapse) cycle." Same loop structure, different direction.

---

**Q16. Inventory Control: "Too many parts → slow production → ramp up production → inventory rises to target." This describes:**

A. A Reinforcing (R) loop driving runaway growth
B. A Balancing (B) loop — the system corrects toward a desired inventory level
C. A delay that causes oscillation
D. A system trap with no solution

✅ **Correct Answer: B**
**Trap Explanation:** Notes use inventory control as a Balancing loop example. The system detects a gap (actual vs. desired inventory) and corrects — ramp up production when inventory is too low, slow down when too high. It SEEKS a goal. That = Balancing loop. Option A is wrong — it doesn't grow exponentially, it corrects.

**Q17. How does a Balancing loop appear in a Behaviour over Time (BOT) graph?**

A. Exponential growth or collapse (upward or downward curve)
B. Flat horizontal line (no change)
C. S-shaped curve

✅ **Correct Answer: D**
**Trap Explanation:** Notes: Balancing loops in BOTs → "resistance to change, recurring cycles, flattening trends." Reinforcing loops → "exponential growth or collapse, steady upward/downward trend." This is a diagram-comprehension trap.

**Q18. A reinforcing loop containing ZERO O's in its CLD. What is the loop type and the expected BOT behaviour?**

A. Balancing loop (B) — Goal-seeking, stabilizing
B. Reinforcing loop (R) — Exponential growth or collapse
C. Balancing loop (B) — Oscillation
D. Reinforcing loop (R) — Goal-seeking, stabilizing

✅ **Correct Answer: B**

**Q19. "S and O are alternatives to + and –." Which of the following correctly translates a link marked 'O'?**

A. As A increases, B also increases (same direction)
B. As A increases, B decreases, OR as A decreases, B increases (opposite direction)
C. There is a delay in the A→B relationship
D. A and B are completely unrelated

✅ **Correct Answer: B**
**Trap Explanation:** Notes: "O's → opposite direction. AT, B↓ / A↓, BT." Both scenarios count as O: A↑→B↓ AND A↓→B↑. Students often only memorize one direction. Also: S = same direction = + = A↑→B↑ OR A↓→B↓.

**Q31. In the Prisoner's Dilemma payoff matrix (Ravi & Vanish), if Ravi CONFESSES and Vanish DENIES, what are the outcomes?**

C. Ravi = 1 year, Vanish = 10 years
D. Ravi = 2 years, Vanish = 2 years

✅ **Correct Answer: C**
**Trap Explanation:** Notes payoff matrix: If one confesses and the other denies → confessor gets 1 year (reduced for cooperation), denier gets 10 years. Ravi confesses, Vanish denies → Ravi = 1 year, Vanish = 10 years. Students often flip this.

---

**Q32. In the Prisoner's Dilemma, the "globally optimal condition" (best for both combined) is:**

A. Both confess (3 years each = 6 years total)
B. One confesses, one denies (1+10 = 11 years total)
C. Both deny (2 years each = 4 years total — minimum combined sentence)
D. Both receive 10 years

✅ **Correct Answer: C**
**Trap Explanation:** Notes: "Global optimal condition — both deny." 2+2 = 4 years total — the minimum combined punishment. However, this is NOT the Nash Equilibrium because neither player can trust the other to deny. Nash Equilibrium (both confess, 3+3=6 years) is worse collectively but stable individually.

---

**Q33. Why is "both deny" NOT the Nash Equilibrium in the Prisoner's Dilemma?**

A. Because denying gives a higher individual payoff than confessing
B. Because if both deny, EITHER player can gain by unilaterally switching to confess (getting 1 year instead of 2) — making "both deny" an unstable state
C. Because both players always cooperate in Game Theory
D. Because the prison sentence doesn't change if one player switches

✅ **Correct Answer: B**
**Trap Explanation:** Nash Equilibrium = no player CAN gain by unilaterally changing strategy. If both deny (2 years each) → one player could confess → gets 1 year (better). So "both deny" is UNSTABLE — it's not Nash Equilibrium. "Both confess" = Nash Equilibrium because switching to deny when the other confesses gives you 10 years (worse). No one gains by deviating.
